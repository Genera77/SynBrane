About SynBrane

SynBrane is an experimental sound engine built to merge tuning theory, chord building, and rhythm generation into a single creative tool.

You sculpt chords using a spiraling circular interface tuned to any temperament — 8-EDO, 10-EDO, 12-EDO, 13-EDO, 15-EDO, 16-EDO, 17-EDO, 19-EDO, 20-EDO, 22-EDO, 24-EDO, 26-EDO, 27-EDO, 31-EDO, Orwell-9, or any Scala tuning — and hear them instantly as either harmony or rhythm.

Harmony Mode

In Harmony Mode, your chord plays as a synth sound:

  • Choose any temperament
  • Select notes on the spiral
  • Pick a root and chord preset
  • Shape the sound with ADSR, waveform, detune, and filter controls
  • Add arpeggiators per chord
  • Preview chords or render full loops from the backend

Harmony Mode acts like a microtonal chord workstation, but built around your tuning system rather than a piano keyboard.

Rhythm Mode — Turning a Chord Into a Beat

Rhythm Mode transforms your chord into a polyrhythmic drum machine driven by harmonic ratios.

Instead of playing pitches, each note becomes a percussive instrument, and the chord’s internal interval structure becomes a rhythmic engine. You’re not just triggering drums — you’re literally slowing the chord down into sub-audio timing, then using those long harmonic cycles to generate pulse patterns.

How it works:

  • Each selected degree becomes a trigger point.
  • A note’s degree is normalized to a fractional position inside one bar, based on the size of the temperament.

The Rhythm × slider slows everything down:

  • 1.00× → normal timing
  • 0.30× → ~3× slower (default)
  • 0.10× → ~10× slower (deep pulses and long polyrhythmic spacing)

This reveals the underlying ratios of the chord as rhythmic shapes.

Degrees map to drum types:

  • Lower degrees → low drums
  • Middle degrees → claps / snares
  • Higher degrees → hats / ticks

Kick + Snare backbone stays locked:

  • Kick on beats 1 and 3
  • Snare on 2 and 4

The full chord becomes a rhythmic texture.

Each note fires at its slowed-down position in the bar, creating a groove determined entirely by:

  • temperament structure
  • chord shape
  • rhythmic slowdown
  • tempo

This produces complex polyrhythms generated directly from harmonic relationships — something impossible in a traditional DAW quantized to 16th notes.

Rhythm Mode essentially asks:
“What if your chord was your beat?”

Looping & Composition

  • Choose between 1 and 5 chords
  • Each chord has its own tuning, notes, root, preset, and arpeggiator
  • Loops repeat 10 times or until stopped
  • Preview loops in the browser or render a WAV through the backend

SynBrane functions as a microtonal groovebox for harmonic and rhythmic experimentation.

Patch System

Save or load complete patches as JSON containing:

  • Global synth settings
  • Tempo and rhythm multiplier
  • Loop chord count
  • All chord notes, tunings, roots, presets, and arpeggiators

Patches behave like portable “scenes” you can reload at any time.
